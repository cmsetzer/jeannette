Events & Observations

This entry contains remarks related to the following subject: Sea Ice 
Water expended during the preceding 24 hours: 35 gallons
Water distilled during the preceding 24 hours: 35 gallons
Coal consumed during the preceding 24 hours: 290 lbs
Coal remaining on hand at noon: 66 tons 608 lbs 

Maximum temperature indicated by B.B. in vacuo = 68.3°

The pumping forward is done by hand at the spar deck bilge pump, and aft by the steam cutters engine 
driving the main engine bilge pump. The steam cutters engine is also used for distilling.

Sounded at noon in 33 fathoms. Muddy bottom. A drift to S.S.E. being indicated by the lead line. Ice 2 1/2 
inches in thickness formed around the edges of the sounding hole since noon yesterday. Sunrise at 3. 
Sunset obscured. At midnight heavy water sky from E.S.E. to S.W. 
The wind mill was mounted in place over the starboard side of the bridge and secured. Carpenters 
engaged in making new pump brake for attaching the same to the shifted spar deck bilge pump in the 
corner of the fire room hatch; and engineer's force occupied in fitting connecting rod &c.
The following named articles were found on the ice about a mile and a half to the southward of the ship. 
7 small pieces of wood 
1 bunch vegetable matter 
1 piece birch bark 
1 small leaf

Weather gloomy all day with almost steady fall of very light snow. Light northerly and westerly winds, 
veering to northward and eastward after noon. Rising barometer and increasing temperature.

Moon 6° 55' N.
