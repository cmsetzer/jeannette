USS Jeannette (1879–1881)

Monday, 8 December 1879

Beset in the pack to the N.W. of Herald Island

Position: No position

Events & Observations

This entry contains remarks related to the following subjects: Aurora Sea Ice 
Latitude by observation at noon: no observation
Longitude by chronometer from forenoon observations: no observation  

Water expended during the preceding 24 hours: using as distilled.
Water distilled during the preceding 24 hours: 40 gallons
Coal consumed during the preceding 24 hours: 187 lbs
Coal remaining on hand at noon: 102 tons 1546 lbs

AM
Clear and cold. Gentle breeze from N'd & E'd. Very brilliant aurora. At 12 sounded in 29 1/2 fathoms. Blue 
mud. Slight drift to the N.W. (mag.). Ship heeling 2 1/2° to starboard. Heavy pressure on young ice to the 
N.E. of ship about 1/2 mile distant. Loud noises from that quarter.

PM
Clear and cold. Hazy about horizon. Gentle breezes from S.E. quarter. Ice to N.E. of ship moving and 
crushing with violence. Washed clothes. Distilling.

Moon 13° S. 
Last quarter
