Water expended during the preceding 24 hours: 35 gallons
Water distilled during the preceding 24 hours: 35 gallons
Coal consumed during the preceding 24 hours: 435 lbs
Coal remaining on hand at noon: 64 tons 1783 lbs 

Maximum temperature indicated by B.B. in vacuo = 84° between 1 and 2pm

The pumping forward is done by hand at the spar deck bilge pump, and aft by the steam cutters engine 
driving the main engine bilge pump. The steam cutter's boiler is also used for distilling.

Sounded at noon in 33 1/2 fathoms. Muddy bottom. A drift to N.N.W. being indicated by the lead line.
Weather gloomy and dull. Sky overcast, with an almost steady fall of light snow. Water sky at times 
between E. and S. Light southerly and easterly winds with slightly falling barometer and pleasant 
temperature. 
At 11.30pm a bear came to within half a mile of the ship, but being pursued, managed to escape.
Carpenters engaged in fitting tins to wind-mill instead of ordinary sails. Crew engaged generally in work 
about the ship.

Moon 24° 20' S. 
Full moon
